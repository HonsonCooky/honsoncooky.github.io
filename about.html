About

I grew up doing musical theatre and music, which probably sounds weird for a Software Engineer. Honestly though, the overlap is bigger than you'd think - structure, timing, knowing when to improvise and when to follow the script. It also meant I got comfortable presenting and communicating early, which helps tremendously.

I was always more of a maths and science person though. I picked up programming in school and took it to university. The further I got into it - Operating Systems, Compilers, Networking - the more it just made sense. In my fourth year I built a BLE mesh network on M5Stack devices. That project stuck with me. Following low-level dictates, making calculated decisions on how the system came together - it aligned with how I think. It was difficult, but the satisfaction of seeing every layer click into place was something I hadn't felt in a long time. That's when I knew systems-level engineering was where I belonged.

Professionally, most of my work has been building and maintaining production systems in TypeScript and C#. Specs, org workflows, the usual. I'm good at it, and it pays the bills. The stuff I actually get excited about is lower down though - systems-level programming, performance, how computers actually work under the hood. Right now that's Go. Eventually it'll be Zig.
